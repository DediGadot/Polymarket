  • Theoretical totals are not equal to realizable PnL: dry-run assumes quoted liquidity and no queue slippage; real fills may be lower.
  • Maker edge was tiny (sub-$1 average signature profit) and intermittently absent; likely sensitive to queue position and microstructure noise.
  • Several very high ROI signatures came from small required capital or thin books; those deserve manual depth checks before trusting headline ROI.
  • BUY/SELL inventory-required signatures can look profitable in scans but may not be actionable without existing inventory and good basis.
